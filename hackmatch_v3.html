🛡️ Hackathon Survival Guide Generator
Click "Prepare Me" and get a personalized hackathon survival plan — what to bring, your dev stack, hour-by-hour strategy, and pitch structure.
🎒 What to Bring
Laptop + charger + backup power bank (minimum 20,000mAh)
Noise-cancelling headphones for deep focus sessions
Healthy snacks: dates, nuts, protein bars — avoid sugar crashes
Offline docs: API references, cheat sheets, design patterns
Business cards or LinkedIn QR code for networking
Change of clothes & hygiene kit for overnight events
💻 Recommended Dev Stack
Frontend: React + Tailwind CSS — fast, beautiful, judge-friendly
Backend: Node.js + Express or FastAPI (Python) — rapid prototyping
AI/ML: HuggingFace models, OpenAI API, or Google Gemini
Database: Supabase or Firebase — serverless, instant setup
Deploy: Vercel (30 seconds) or Netlify for instant live demo
⏱️ Hour 1–24 Strategy
Hours 1–2: Team alignment, problem selection, define MVP scope
Hours 3–8: Core feature development — build the demo, not the product
Hours 9–14: Integration, bug fixing, basic UI polish
Hours 15–20: Slide deck, demo video, pitch narrative refinement
Hours 21–23: Sleep! A rested mind pitches 40% more effectively
Hour 24: Final run-through, tech check, prepare backup demo
🎤 Pitch Structure (5-minute formula)
Min 0–1: Hook — start with a powerful problem story or statistic
Min 1–2: Solution — show your demo first, explain second
Min 2–3: Impact & market — why this matters at scale
Min 3–4: Tech stack & innovation — what makes yours unique
Min 4–5: Team, roadmap, Vision 2030 alignment (for GCC events)
